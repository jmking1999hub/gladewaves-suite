Who We Are

A Louisiana community nonprofit — Metro New Orleans, Est. 2018

Gladewaves was formed to give direct aid to people who need it. "Boots on the ground" is our mantra. Our highly skilled staff and Board of Directors are hands-on in every aspect of our non-profit. Together we work to bring resources to the Greater New Orleans area that are both useful and needed.

Every member of our staff is well versed in every aspect of our programming and this makes us stronger as a team and smarter as an organization. Our commitment is to continue working to develop programs that will have a direct impact on improving the lives of the people in our community.
Our Mission
"Gladewaves believes that all people have the right to food, shelter, education, companionship, and the tools necessary to ensure a productive life."

Our Four Programs

Serving Metro New Orleans year-round

📦 Big Red Box Program
Gladewaves' Big Red Box initiative addresses food insecurity in Metro New Orleans by providing free food pantries to underprivileged and at-risk communities with diverse demographics. We involve locals in creating and maintaining a coalition to fight hunger, in collaboration with local churches, schools, and police stations.

"We encourage everyone to take what they need and give what they can."

🤝 HandUp Program
Gladewaves "Hand Up" program provides start-up housewares to individuals and families leaving a transitional housing program and moving into permanent housing. We work with Unity for the Homeless, Hotel Hope, and the Innocence Project to identify people in need.

Each box is valued at $500 and contains items necessary to furnish a kitchen, bathroom, and bedroom(s) along with a $100 grocery gift card.
👴 Senior Outreach Program
Monthly free luncheon and lecture series for senior citizens in Metro New Orleans, September through June. Open to anyone 55+ for a hot meal and guest speakers. Now serving six locations.

⚖️ Legal Precedent
Federal: Bill Emerson Good Samaritan Food Donation Act (42 U.S.C. § 1791) already protects food donors from civil and criminal liability when donating in good faith to nonprofits.

City precedents: New York City (Local Law 52, 2021), Denver (Good Food Ordinance), San Francisco (food recovery hierarchy), Los Angeles (food recovery ordinance).

The gap: Most N.O. businesses don't know federal protections exist. A city ordinance creates clarity and signals city support — removing perceived risk entirely.
